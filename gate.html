⚙️ About GATE 2026

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering

  • Conducted by IITs and IISc on rotation every year
  • Used for MTech/MS admissions + PSU recruitment
  • Score is valid for 3 years from the date of result
  • Computer-based test — 3 hours, 65 questions
  • Average 10–12 lakh candidates appear every year
  • GATE 2026 will be conducted by IIT Guwahati

✅ Eligibility

EducationBE/BTech/BSc (Research)/BS in relevant subject (final year eligible)
Also EligibleMSc, MA, MCA, MTech (for respective papers)
Age LimitNo age limit for GATE exam
AttemptsUnlimited — can appear every year
NationalityIndian + Candidates from Nepal, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Ethiopia, UAE also eligible

📝 GATE Exam Pattern

GATE Paper Structure

  • Total: 65 questions — 100 marks — 3 hours
  • Engineering Mathematics — ~13 marks (compulsory)
  • General Aptitude — 15 marks (compulsory for all papers)
  • Subject-specific questions — 72 marks
  • MCQ: -1/3 negative marking | NAT: No negative marking
  • MSQ (Multiple Select): No negative marking

Scoring & Cutoffs

  • GATE Score = normalized marks on a scale of 0–1000
  • General cutoff (CSE): typically 25–30 marks out of 100
  • PSU cutoffs are higher — ISRO/ONGC expect 600+ GATE score
  • IIT MTech admission: 700+ score preferred for top IITs
  • Result usually in March; score card valid 3 years
