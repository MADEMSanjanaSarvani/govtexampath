🏛️ About UPSC CSE

India's most prestigious civil services examination

  • Conducted by Union Public Service Commission every year
  • Selects for IAS, IPS, IFS, IRS and 20+ other All India Services
  • Only 0.1–0.2% of applicants clear — requires 1–3 years of serious preparation
  • IAS officers get bungalow, car, staff and huge non-monetary perks
  • Maximum 6 attempts for General category (9 for OBC, unlimited for SC/ST)

✅ Eligibility

EducationAny Bachelor's Degree from a recognized university (final year students can also apply)

OBC21–35 years (+3 years relaxation)

NationalityIndian Citizen (for IAS/IPS); some services allow OCI

📝 Exam Pattern — 3 Stages

Stage 1: Prelims (Screening)

  • Paper 1: GS — 100 Qs, 200 marks, 2 hrs
  • Paper 2: CSAT — 80 Qs, 200 marks, 2 hrs (qualifying only — need 33%)
  • Negative marking: -0.66 per wrong answer
  • Only GS Paper 1 marks count for cut-off
  • Usually held in June every year

Stage 2: Mains (Written)

  • 9 papers total — Essay, GS 1-4, Optional (2 papers), Language papers
  • GS 1–4: 250 marks each = 1000 marks total
  • Essay: 250 marks | Optional: 500 marks
  • Language papers: qualifying only
  • Total merit marks: 1750 (Mains) + 275 (Interview)

Stage 3: Personality Test

  • 275 marks interview at UPSC Bhawan, New Delhi
  • Tests personality, leadership, awareness — not bookish knowledge
  • Board of 5 members, 25–45 minutes long
  • Final merit = Mains Total + Interview = 2025 marks
  • Service allocation based on final rank

🏛️ IAS — Indian Administrative Service

Collector, DM, Secretary — ₹56,100–₹2,50,000/month + bungalow + car + staff

👮 IPS — Indian Police Service

SP, DIG, DGP — ₹56,100–₹2,25,000/month + official residence + security

🌳 IFS — Indian Foreign Service

Diplomat, Ambassador — ₹56,100+ with foreign posting allowances

💰 IRS — Indian Revenue Service

Income Tax & Customs — ₹56,100–₹2,25,000/month + ₹10L HRA city allowance

📻 IIS — Indian Information Service

⏱️ Realistic Preparation Plan

  • Minimum 12–14 months of dedicated preparation
  • Start with NCERT books (6-12) — foundation is everything
  • Read The Hindu or Indian Express daily from Day 1
  • Choose optional subject in Month 3 based on interest + success rate
  • Write 2 Mains-style answers daily from Month 6 onwards
