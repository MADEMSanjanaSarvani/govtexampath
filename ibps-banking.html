🏦 Banking Exams at a Glance

🏦 IBPS PO

  • Probationary Officer in 11 Public Sector Banks
  • Age: 20–30 years | Grad required
  • Prelims → Mains → Interview
  • Salary: ₹52,000+/month (all inclusive)
  • ~4,000 vacancies annually

💳 IBPS Clerk

  • Clerical Cadre in 11 Public Sector Banks
  • Age: 20–28 years | Grad required
  • Prelims → Mains (no interview)
  • Salary: ₹26,000–₹34,000/month
  • ~6,000+ vacancies annually

🏛️ SBI PO

  • Probationary Officer in State Bank of India
  • Age: 21–30 years | Grad required
  • Prelims → Mains → GD + Interview
  • Salary: ₹64,000+/month (all inclusive)
  • ~2,000 vacancies annually

💰 SBI Clerk

  • Junior Associate in State Bank of India
  • Age: 20–28 years | Grad required
  • Prelims → Mains (no interview)
  • Salary: ₹29,000–₹38,000/month
  • ~8,000+ vacancies annually

🏦 RBI Grade B

  • Officer in Reserve Bank of India
  • Age: 21–30 years | Grad (60% marks needed)
  • Phase 1 → Phase 2 → Interview
  • Salary: ₹1,02,000+/month (all inclusive)
  • ~200–300 vacancies annually

💼 IBPS SO

  • Specialist Officer (IT, HR, Law, Marketing, Agriculture)
  • Age: 20–30 years | Relevant degree required
  • Prelims → Mains → Interview
  • Salary: ₹52,000+/month
  • ~500–1000 vacancies annually

📝 IBPS PO Exam Pattern (Most Popular)

Prelims (Qualifying)

  • English Language — 30 Qs, 30 marks, 20 min
  • Quantitative Aptitude — 35 Qs, 35 marks, 20 min
  • Reasoning Ability — 35 Qs, 35 marks, 20 min
  • Total: 100 Qs, 100 marks, 1 hour
  • Negative marking: -0.25 per wrong answer

Mains (Merit Based)

  • Reasoning & Computer — 45 Qs, 60 marks, 60 min
  • English Language — 35 Qs, 40 marks, 40 min
  • Data Analysis & Interpretation — 35 Qs, 60 marks, 45 min
  • General Economy & Banking Awareness — 40 Qs, 40 marks, 35 min
  • English Descriptive — Essay + Letter, 25 marks, 30 min
