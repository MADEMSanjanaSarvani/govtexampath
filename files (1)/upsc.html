🏛️ Exam Overview

What is UPSC CSE?

The Civil Services Examination by UPSC is India's most prestigious exam. It selects officers for IAS, IPS, IFS and other All India Services and Central Services. Conducted annually, it has 3 stages: Prelims, Mains and Interview.

🥇 Services You Can Get

🏛️ IAS

Indian Administrative Service — District Collector, Secretary, Chief Secretary. Highest administrative post in India.

👮 IPS

Indian Police Service — SP, DIG, IG, DGP. Oversee law enforcement at district, state and national level.

🌍 IFS

Indian Foreign Service — Ambassador, Consul General, High Commissioner. Represent India internationally.

💰 IRS

Indian Revenue Service (IT & Customs) — Income Tax Officer, Commissioner. Manage tax and customs administration.

📊 IAAS

Indian Audit & Accounts Service — Audit revenue, expenditure of central govt. CAG offices across India.

🏭 IES/ISS

Indian Economic Service / Statistical Service — Policy formulation, economic research in central ministries.

📚 Complete Syllabus

📝 Prelims – GS Paper I

  • Current Events (National & International)
  • Indian History & National Movement
  • Indian & World Geography
  • Indian Polity & Governance
  • Economic & Social Development
  • Environment & Ecology
  • General Science

🧮 Prelims – CSAT (Paper II)

  • Reading Comprehension
  • Logical Reasoning & Analytical Ability
  • Basic Numeracy (Class X level)
  • Data Interpretation
  • Decision Making
  • English Language Comprehension

⚡ Qualifying paper – 33% cutoff

✍️ Mains – Essay (Paper I)

  • Two essays – 1000–1200 words each
  • Section A: Socio-economic, political
  • Section B: Philosophical, abstract
  • Total: 250 marks
  • Time: 3 hours

📖 Mains – GS Papers I–IV

  • GS I: History, Geography, Society
  • GS II: Polity, Governance, IR
  • GS III: Economy, Environment, Security
  • GS IV: Ethics, Integrity, Aptitude
  • 250 marks each

📘 Mains – Optional Subject

  • Choose 1 subject from 48 options
  • 2 papers × 250 marks each = 500 marks
  • Popular: History, Sociology, PSIR, Geography
  • Technical: Mathematics, Physics, Law

🎤 Personality Test (Interview)

  • 275 marks total
  • Board of senior IAS officers
  • Tests mental alertness, integrity, leadership
  • Duration: 30–45 minutes
  • DAF (bio-data) based questions
🎯 Step-by-Step Preparation Strategy
1

NCERT Books Class 6–12 (Months 1–3)

Start with History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Science NCERTs. This is the non-negotiable foundation every topper builds on first.

2

Standard Reference Books (Months 3–8)

Laxmikanth for Polity, Bipin Chandra for History, Ramesh Singh for Economy, GC Leong for Geography. Read each at least twice.

3

Daily Newspaper (From Day 1)

Read The Hindu or Indian Express 1–2 hours daily. Focus on governance, economy, international affairs, environment. Make concise notes.

4

Choose Optional Subject Early (Month 1–2)

Select based on your graduation background and interest. Most toppers pick Geography, Sociology, Anthropology, PSIR or History.

5

Answer Writing Practice (From Month 4)

Practice writing 150 and 250 word answers daily. Join a test series. Answer writing is the single biggest differentiator in Mains.

6

Mock Tests and PYQs (Month 8 onwards)

Solve last 10 years Prelims papers. Join a Mains test series. Analyze mistakes. Revise weak areas repeatedly.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
How many attempts are allowed for UPSC CSE? ▾
General category: 6 attempts up to age 32. OBC: 9 attempts up to age 35. SC/ST: unlimited attempts up to age 37. PwBD: 9 attempts up to age 42.
What is the minimum qualification for UPSC CSE? ▾
You need a Bachelor's degree from any recognized university in any discipline. Students in their final year are also eligible to apply (they must submit proof before Mains).
How long does UPSC preparation typically take? ▾
Most successful candidates take 1–3 years. A dedicated full-time preparation of 12–15 months is considered sufficient for the first serious attempt. Part-time preparation alongside a job typically takes 2–3 years.
Is coaching necessary for UPSC CSE? ▾
No. Many toppers have cleared UPSC without coaching using self-study. However, coaching can help with structure, guidance and test series. The key is quality of study, not the mode. Free resources like YouTube (Unacademy, StudyIQ) are excellent alternatives.
What is the UPSC Prelims cutoff? ▾
The Prelims cutoff varies each year. For General category it typically ranges between 88–110 marks out of 200 (GS Paper I). CSAT has a fixed qualifying cutoff of 33% (66/200).
Can I prepare for UPSC while working? ▾
Yes, many candidates prepare while working. Dedicate at least 4–5 hours daily on weekdays and 8–10 hours on weekends. Use morning hours for serious study, commute time for current affairs. Take leave during the final 2–3 months before Prelims.
