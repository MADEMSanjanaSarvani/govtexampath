What is APPSC?
Andhra Pradesh's gateway to state government careers

The Andhra Pradesh Public Service Commission (APPSC) is the state body that recruits candidates for government positions across Andhra Pradesh. If you're from Visakhapatnam (Vizag), Vijayawada, Tirupati or anywhere in AP — APPSC is your most direct route to a stable, high-paying government job in your home state.


APPSC conducts 8+ exams per year — from the prestigious Group 1 (equivalent to state IAS) all the way to entry-level Group 4 and Panchayat Secretary posts. One of APPSC's biggest advantages over national exams is the heavy focus on Andhra Pradesh history, culture, economy and current affairs — which gives local students from Vizag, Guntur and Tirupati a major edge.


The exams are conducted in Telugu and English. Reservation benefits apply as per AP state rules — OBC (BC-A, BC-B, BC-C, BC-D), SC, ST, EWS categories all have reserved seats.

📋 Group 1 Selection Process
Step-by-step breakdown of how toppers get selected
1
Preliminary Exam — Screening
150 MCQ questions, 150 marks, 2.5 hours. General Studies only. This is just qualifying — you need to pass it to get to Mains. Negative marking applies (1/3 mark deducted per wrong answer).
2
Mains Exam — Core Stage
6 descriptive papers, 1500 total marks. AP history, culture and current affairs is heavily tested. Essay paper in Telugu/English is Paper 1. This stage decides your rank.
3
Interview / Personality Test
75 marks. Covers your personality, awareness of AP issues, governance knowledge and communication. Conducted in Amaravati/Vijayawada. Telugu and English both accepted.
4
Document Verification
Keep all originals ready — degree certificate, caste certificate (BC/SC/ST), nativity (AP domicile), residence proof from Visakhapatnam district.
5
Final Posting
Based on merit rank + your post preferences. North AP districts (Vizag, Vizianagaram, Srikakulam) posting available. You can request home district posting.
🎯 APPSC Preparation Strategy for Vizag Students
Tested tips from APPSC toppers from North Andhra
📜
Start with AP History & Culture
Satavahanas, Vijayanagara Empire, Nizam period, AP States Reorganization Act (2014) — these come every year. Read AP State Board Social textbooks (free on AP SCERT site).
📰
Read Eenadu / Sakshi Daily
Both Eenadu and Sakshi have free current affairs sections online. 10–15 questions every APPSC exam come from AP state news. 30 minutes daily is enough.
🏛️
Master YSR Government Schemes
Memorize all AP govt schemes: YSR Rythu Bharosa, Aarogyasri, Jagananna Ammavodi, Jagananna Vidya Deevena, YSR Housing — asked every year in Group 1 & 2.
📚
Use AP SCERT Textbooks
AP State Board books (Class 6–10) are the best source for history, geography and science for APPSC. They're free to download from the AP SCERT official website.
✍️
Practice Telugu Answer Writing
Group 1 Mains Paper 1 is descriptive essay in Telugu or English. Start writing 200-word answers from Month 2. Use simple, clear Telugu — examiners reward clarity over complex words.
🎯
APPSC-Specific Mock Tests
Use Sakshi Education, Manabadi, or Adda247 Telugu mock tests — they have AP-specific questions. Aim for 2 full mocks per week from Month 3 onwards.
